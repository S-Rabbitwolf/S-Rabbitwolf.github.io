The Pacific Rhododendron - Beauty in Washington

Images courtesy of the Rhododendron Species Botanical Gardens

The Pacific Rhododendron is the State Flower of Washington, an evergreen shrub which can grow up to 9 meters tall. It is known across the Pacific west coast for its bright coloration and presence on mountain-faces along the coast.
The flowers of the shrub are known for their particular vibrancy, standing out among the large leaves upon which they rest.
The pacific rhodendron is well regarded for its variance in colors and magnitude.

The Pacific Rhodendron is an evergreen shrub native to the western coast of North America, particularly near Oregon and Washington. Known for its clusters of pink and purple flowers, it usually blooms from April to June along coastal hillsides. The flowers are known for their vibrancy, their large leaves, and resliency given the variable climate in which they grow.
