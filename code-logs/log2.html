In web development, it's important to utilize the various tools that CSS has to offer in regards to precise units of measurement. The same can be said for other units of absolute measurement, such as millimeters (mm), points (pt), and inches (in). However, in order to differentiate between different assets, it becomes necessary to understand the difference between absolute and relative properties of measurement. Why The Difference Is Important

Absolute values in and of themselves are easy to understand the purpose of. By assigning defined (absolute) values to certain assets, it becomes much easier to structure your website's content in digestible ways. With this knowledge, you can think of the purpose of relative measurement to be supplying context to these measurements. This is both for readability and for structural integrity. Take the measurement of px and pt for example. A pixel is 1/96th of 1 inch, and a point is 1/72nd of 1 inch. There are various reasons why you may want to use px over pt and vice versa, especially if the content of your page is not specifically tied to text. Say you want to have an image cropped onto your website, but the text hugs it far too closely. If you were to only use absolute values for this purpose, you would be able to have the image put onto your site easily, but it would be needlessly difficult to adjust the text relative to its position.
