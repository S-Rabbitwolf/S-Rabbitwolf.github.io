Utilization of HTML Semantic Elements

Semantic arguments in terms of HTML prompts are in the name. The nature of semantic arguments denotes that they define the content of what it entails. For example, the img tag is semantic due to its use as an identifier for images on a page. Non-semantic arguments, like div, h(x), etc. do not denote specific content. In short, you can think of it like this. The non-semantic arguments give the page a skeleton, and the semantic arguments utilize that skeleton to display content This information can be combined to give the website its basic shape and structure. These elements can be combined at will, with minimal restriction.
For example. An image tag can also be anchored to a link, making the image be clickable to take you somewhere else. In this instance, it will take you to the location of the file itself on imgur.com.
